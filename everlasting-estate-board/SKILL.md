# Everlasting Estate Board Skill

## Purpose

Transform a user's dream-home intake responses into a luxury architectural concept board in the style of an editorial estate presentation: part floor-plan fantasy, part lifestyle vision board, part family legacy mood board.

This skill is designed for Everlasting Homes Building Group / My Everlasting Home-style outputs, especially SCIP/resilient-home concept boards.

## Important disclaimer

Always treat the result as conceptual design direction only. Do not present the output as code-compliant, permit-ready, construction-ready, engineered, surveyed, or final architectural documentation. Include a short disclaimer in generated copy when appropriate:

> Conceptual lifestyle and architectural direction board only. Not construction-ready plans, engineering documents, or permit drawings.

## When to use this skill

Use this skill when the user asks for any of the following:

- Dream-home concept board
- Estate aesthetic blueprint
- Luxury architectural presentation board
- Family legacy estate board
- SCIP home concept board
- Home vision board based on an intake form
- Two-image board: floor-plan fantasy + lifestyle vision board
- My Everlasting Home-style board
- Reference image recreation in the format of a luxury estate board

## Core board philosophy

The board should feel like:

- Luxury architecture editorial
- High-end real estate presentation
- Family office legacy planning document
- Soft architectural blueprint, but not technical drafting
- Warm, timeless, premium, calm, generational
- A concept board that maps how a future family estate could feel and function

Avoid:

- Generic AI mood board clutter
- Overly technical construction drawings
- Cartoonish renderings
- Fake permit language
- Cheap real estate flyer aesthetics
- Overcrowded collage layouts
- Unreadable text
- Random unrelated luxury objects

For a full board, generate these sections in order:

## Board naming rules

Use a polished estate name. If the user gives a family name, use:

- The [Family Name] Residence
- The [Family Name] Estate
- Casa [Family Name]
- [Family Name] Legacy House

If no family name is provided, create a title based on the location or concept, such as:

- The Hill Country Residence
- The Banyan Estate
- The Lotus Courtyard Estate
- The Coastal Legacy House

## Visual layout rules

Default aspect ratio: ultra-wide horizontal presentation board, approximately 16:9 or wider.

Layout hierarchy:

- Top 25–35%: cinematic exterior hero image spanning full width
- Upper middle: floor-plan fantasy panels and site-plan map
- Middle/lower: architectural vignette image strip
- Lower: lifestyle image strip
- Bottom: SCIP/resilience icons, short benefit blurbs, brand/footer line

Typography:

- Elegant serif for title
- Small caps or spaced lettering for section headers
- Minimal sans-serif for labels
- Avoid long paragraphs
- Keep text sparse and legible

Preferred palette:

- Warm ivory
- Soft stone
- Limestone beige
- Charcoal ink
- Muted sage
- Warm oak
- Soft gold accents

## Board section details

### 1. Estate Title

Format example:
THE MOCK RESIDENCE

Use uppercase, spaced luxury editorial styling.

### 2. Estate Subtitle

Format example:
A MODERN FAMILY ESTATE BUILT FOR BEAUTY, WELLNESS, AND LEGACY.

Keep it short and emotionally clear.

### 3. Legacy Statement

A short 2–4 line statement in a refined voice.

Example:
Built for a life well lived — a home that balances timeless design, modern wellness, family connection, and enduring craftsmanship.

### 4. Hero Exterior Prompt

Create a cinematic exterior prompt based on the family's region, architecture, and lifestyle.

Always specify:

- Camera angle
- Time of day
- Materials
- Landscaping
- Lighting
- Atmosphere
- Luxury residential realism
- No text unless the image model handles text well

### 11. SCIP / Resilience Advantage Strip

Use 4–6 benefit modules. Each should have a short headline and one sentence.

Suggested modules:

- Energy Efficiency: Superior insulation performance designed for year-round comfort.
- Strength + Resilience: Concrete core construction supports long-term durability.
- Quiet + Comfortable: Dense concrete mass helps create a calmer interior environment.
- Healthy Living: Materials and planning can support cleaner, more intentional living.
- Built to Endure: Designed for generational use, lower maintenance, and lasting value.

Do not overstate claims. Avoid guaranteeing disaster-proof, fireproof, hurricane-proof, mold-proof, or insurance savings.

Examples:

- A FAMILY LEGACY OF BEAUTY, WELLNESS & MEANING.
- STRONGER. SMARTER. QUIETER. BUILT FOR GENERATIONS.

## Image prompt formula

When creating a final image prompt, use this formula:

Create an ultra-wide luxury architectural presentation board for [ESTATE TITLE]. The board is a conceptual dream-home estate blueprint and lifestyle vision board, not construction documents. Top section: [HERO EXTERIOR]. Middle section: refined floor-plan fantasy panels labeled [MAIN FLOOR], [UPPER FLOOR], [WELLNESS LEVEL], and [ESTATE SITE PLAN]. Include clean, soft architectural plan graphics with numbered zones, pale stone paper texture, fine black linework, muted landscaping, and minimal labels. Lower section: two horizontal strips of photorealistic lifestyle vignettes showing [INTERIOR SPACES] and [LIFESTYLE MOMENTS]. Bottom section: SCIP/resilience advantage icons with short refined captions. Use elegant serif typography, small caps labels, warm ivory background, limestone beige, muted sage, charcoal ink, soft gold accents, high-end editorial layout, refined spacing, premium family legacy mood. Keep all text minimal and legible.

## Text handling note

If the target image model struggles with text, generate the board in two passes:

1. Generate image-only visual board with blank label areas.
2. Add typography, captions, icons, and labels in HTML/CSS, Figma, Canva, or SVG.

For web apps, prefer actual HTML text overlays instead of baked-in AI text whenever possible.

## Quality checklist

Before finalizing, verify:

- The board feels premium and calm.
- The estate title matches the family or concept.
- The plan sections are conceptual, not technical.
- The lifestyle sections reflect the user’s actual intake.
- SCIP claims are careful and not overstated.
- The board has enough white space.
- The text is minimal.
- The visual hierarchy matches: hero → plans → lifestyle → resilience → footer.
- A conceptual disclaimer is included when appropriate.
