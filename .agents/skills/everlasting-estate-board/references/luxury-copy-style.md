# Luxury Copy Style Guide

All text generated for the estate board — estate name, narrative, tagline, section labels — follows this voice and style. This is the editorial standard for My Everlasting Home.

---

## Voice

**Publication reference:** Architectural Digest meets a private family letter. The reader is a discerning family building their legacy home. They are educated, unhurried, and values-driven. They distrust hype. They respond to specificity, restraint, and warmth.

**Tone adjectives:** Grounded. Luminous. Unhurried. Precise. Warm without sentiment. Aspirational without pretension.

**Tone anti-adjectives:** Salesy. Hyperbolic. Generic. Corporate. Casual. Trendy.

---

## Estate Name

**Function:** The estate name is a proper noun — it names this specific property for this specific family. It should feel like it was discovered, not invented.

**Formula:** `[The/La/Casa/etc.] + [evocative noun or place reference] + [optional typological word]`

**Good examples:**
- The Hawthorn Reserve
- Ridgeline House
- Casa del Viento
- The Birchwood Estate
- Clearwater Farm
- Thornfield House
- The Meridian

**Bad examples (avoid):**
- Dream Home (too generic)
- The Luxury Estate (descriptive, not a name)
- Everlasting Haven (too on-the-nose with the brand)
- Casa Grande (overused)
- My Perfect Home (first-person, informal)

**Rules:**
- 2–4 words maximum
- Reflects the aesthetic style, climate region, or family character — not the brand
- Monogram = first letter of each meaningful word (skip "The", "La", "Casa")
- If Mediterranean style → lean toward Spanish/Italian names
- If Farmhouse → lean toward agrarian/pastoral names (Clearwater, Thornfield, The Meadow)
- If Modern Organic → nature-material hybrid names (Ridgeline, The Birchwood, Stone & Fern)
- If Traditional → classic English estate names (The Ashwood, Halcyon House)
- If Minimalist → crisp, single-concept names (The Meridian, Limen, The Form)

## Estate Subtitle

**Function:** One sentence that names the essential character of this estate and who it's for.

**Length:** 10–18 words exactly.

**Must include:** at least one of — family reference, lifestyle priority, place/climate hint, or construction ethos.

**Template pattern (vary freely):**
`A [adjective] [estate type] [built/designed/conceived] for [specific family value], [specific family value], and [generational sentiment].`

**Good examples:**
- "A multigenerational wellness estate conceived for daily beauty, deep roots, and enduring legacy."
- "A coastal farmhouse retreat designed for slow mornings, homeschool rhythms, and family legacy."
- "A contemporary sanctuary built around wellness, gathering, and the art of living well."

**Bad examples:**
- "Your dream home, beautifully designed." (too short, too generic)
- "A home for a family that loves luxury and has high standards." (informal, vague)

---

## Estate Narrative

**Function:** The board's descriptive paragraph — the only long-form copy. Reads like the first paragraph of an AD feature.

**Length:** 2 sentences, 40–65 words total.

**Sentence 1:** Set the scene — climate/region, architectural style, materiality, scale.
**Sentence 2:** Deliver the meaning — what makes this estate *theirs*, the priorities it embodies, the life it enables.

**Must reference:**
- The specific climate or region from form data (not "a temperate climate" — use their actual answer)
- The aesthetic material palette (board-formed concrete, terracotta tile, white oak, etc.)
- At least one lifestyle priority by name or implication

**Forbidden phrases:**
- "dream home" / "dream estate"
- "state-of-the-art"
- "luxurious amenities"
- "perfect for entertaining"
- "no expense spared"
- Any use of "stunning," "beautiful," "gorgeous," "amazing"
- Passive voice ("was designed to") — use active ("designed to", "built around")

**Good example:**
> Set in the sun-drenched hill country of central Texas, this organic modern estate spans 7,200 square feet of board-formed concrete and warm cedar — a residence whose every room opens deliberately toward the land. Anchored in wellness and slow living, the home was conceived as a private sanctuary for a multigenerational family who intends to stay.

**Bad example:**
> This stunning luxury home is perfect for a family that wants to enjoy a beautiful lifestyle. It has amazing amenities and gorgeous design throughout.

---

## Editorial Note

**Function:** A short handwritten-note style line that appears in the masthead, right-aligned. Like a caption on a mood board pinned to a wall.

**Length:** 8–14 words.

**Tone:** Intimate, first-person optional, warm. The voice of someone who knew this family when they designed it.

**Good examples:**
- "Built for a life well lived — room by room, season by season."
- "For the family that chooses roots over convenience."
- "This is the home your children will remember."
- "Designed for the life you already know how to live."
- "Where the morning is slow and the structure is permanent."

**Bad examples:**
- "A great home for a great family!" (exclamation marks forbidden)
- "AI-generated estate concept for client approval." (too transactional)
- "Built with SCIP technology for maximum performance." (too technical)

---

## Footer Tagline

**Function:** Gold italic serif, center of footer. A quiet family motto or generational sentiment. This is the last thing a viewer reads.

**Length:** 4–7 words.

**Tone:** Timeless. Could be carved in stone.

**Good examples:**
- "This is our legacy."
- "Rooted in beauty. Built to last."
- "For now, and for after."
- "The home they will return to."
- "Built once. Lived in always."

**Bad examples:**
- "Powered by My Everlasting Home AI" (brand, not sentiment)
- "An amazing place to live!" (tone violation)
- "Design the estate. Build the life." (that's the hero headline — don't repeat)

---

## Section Labels

All section labels follow the same visual and copy pattern:

`── [LABEL IN SMALL CAPS] ──`

- `LIFE LIVED BEAUTIFULLY` (interior strip)
- `THE [ESTATE NAME] LIFESTYLE` (outdoor strip — uses actual estate name)
- `BUILT BETTER. BUILT TO LAST.` (SCIP section — fixed, never changes)
- Floor plan headers: `MAIN FLOOR`, `UPPER FLOOR`, `LOWER · WELLNESS LEVEL`, `ESTATE SITE PLAN`

**Rules:**
- All caps, tracking 0.2em+
- Always flanked by thin gold rule lines
- Never sentence case in labels
- The lifestyle strip label uses the actual estate name (e.g. `THE HAWTHORN LIFESTYLE`)

---

## Numbers & Measurements

- Square footage: always formatted with comma (`7,200 sq ft` not `7200`)
- Bedroom count: spell out for 1–4 (`four bedrooms`), numeral for 5+ (`6-bedroom`)
- Room dimensions: not used in board copy — implied by zone size in floor plans
- Budget: never stated in copy — omit entirely from all visible board text

---

## What Claude Must Never Do

- Invent features not present in the form data (don't add a wine cellar if `wellnessSuite` is false and it wasn't selected)
- Use superlatives without specificity ("the finest" → say what makes it fine)
- Repeat the same adjective twice in one field
- Write in second person for the narrative ("your home") — use third person ("the estate", "the residence", "the home")
- Reference My Everlasting Home, DALL-E, AI, or any technology brand in visible board copy
